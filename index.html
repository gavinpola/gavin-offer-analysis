Beyond the Numbers

Financial analysis tells part of the story. Here's what it doesn't capture:

Co-Founder Mentorship

Reporting directly to Matt Rogers (Nest co-founder, sold to Google for $3.2B). This is an MBA-level education in company building from someone who's done it at the highest level.

Leadership at 23

Leading AI and GTM at a Series C company. Most people don't get this scope until their 30s. The experience compounds over a career.

"Built This Company" Credential

If Mill succeeds, being early leadership team is career-defining. If starting a company later, this is the best possible preparation.

Mission Alignment

Gavin has already built an entire AI workflow system for Mill on his own time. This isn't just a job - it's genuine passion for the mission.

BCG Brand Value

"BCG Consultant" is a universally recognized credential that opens doors. Leaving early means not fully capturing this optionality.

Startup Risk

~90% of startups fail. Even well-funded ones with great founders. The equity could be worth zero.

Why This Fits Gavin's Profile

  • Entrepreneurial track record: Co-founded Gryphon Ventures at 19, raised $1.05M from 21 LPs, invested in 5 startups. He understands startup risk/reward.
  • 5-year goal: Building or investing in startups. Mill is directly on this path; BCG is a detour.
  • Risk tolerance: As a fund manager who wrote checks into early-stage companies, he's comfortable with illiquid, high-risk assets.
  • Already committed: Built AI tools for Mill before even accepting the offer. This level of conviction is rare.

Important Caveats

This analysis makes simplifying assumptions. Here's what it excludes:

What's NOT included - Mill Side

  • Future equity refreshes (common at startups as you take on more responsibility)
  • Potential performance bonuses or GTM commission structure
  • Salary increases over time
  • Potential acceleration if Mill is acquired

What's NOT included - BCG Side

  • Promotions beyond Project Leader (Principal, Partner track)
  • MBA sponsorship value (~$200K+)
  • Exit opportunities (PE, Corp Strategy at $300K+ roles)
  • Superior benefits (100% health premiums, 5% profit sharing)

Key Assumptions

  • 20% dilution in future rounds (could be more or less)
  • Full 4-year vesting (leaving early means less equity)
  • BCG progression: Consultant Year 2, PL Years 3-4
  • No taxes modeled (actual take-home will be lower)
  • Equity is illiquid until exit event (IPO or acquisition)

Recommendation

Accept the Mill Offer
Middle Option: $157,500 + 133,818 shares

Why?

  1. Role is exceptional: Co-founder direct report with AI + GTM leadership. This goes to 5-8 year experienced candidates, not 23-year-olds.
  2. Career alignment: Gavin's 5-year goal is startups. This is the most direct path.
  3. Founders are proven: Nest team built and sold a company for $3.2B. They know how to do this.
  4. Timing is right: Series C with Amazon partnership. This is the growth inflection point.
  5. Conviction is genuine: He's already building for Mill. You can't fake that kind of passion.

The math requires ~6x exit to break even with BCG. That's aggressive but achievable for a well-positioned company with Nest-pedigree founders.

But honestly? If this were purely about maximizing expected value, you'd stay at BCG. The case for Mill is about building something meaningful, learning from world-class founders, and positioning for a career in startups.
